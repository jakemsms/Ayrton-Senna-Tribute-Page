Ayrton Senna da Silva

The fastest driver of his era

History of Ayrton Senna

  • 1960 - Born in Sao Paulo, Brazil
  • 1977 - South American Kart Championship winner
  • 1981 - Formula Ford 1600 Championship winner
  • 1982 - British and European Formula Ford 2000 Championships winner
  • 1983 - British Formula 3 Championship winner
  • 1983 - Tests for Williams, McLaren, Brabham and Toleman F1 teams
  • 1984 - First F1 season in Toleman team (best result: second place in Monaco Grand Prix)
  • 1985 - Became a driver of Lotus F1 team (two wins in Portugal and Belgium, seven pole positions)
  • 1986 - Two wins in Spain and USA F1 GPs, eight pole positions
  • 1987 - Two wins in Monaco and USA F1 GPs, one pole position
  • 1988 - Changed his team to McLaren and won his first F1 World Championship (eight wins and thirteen pole positions
  • 1989 - Runner-up in F1 season after tough fight with his teammate Alain Prost (six wins)
  • 1990 - Second F1 World Champion Title secured after collision with Prost in the last race of the season (six wins)
  • 1991 - Third and last F1 World Champion Title (seven wins)
  • 1992 - Fourth place in F1 season (three wins)
  • 1993 - Second place in F1 season (five wins)
  • 1994 - Changed his team to Williams
  • 1994 - Tragic accident on the Imola track and death in hospital in Bologna, Italy

Ayrton Senna F1 Records

Accurate as of the end of the 2015 season

  • Most wins leading throughout a Grand Prix: 19
  • Most consecutive pole positions: 8
  • Most consecutive front row starts: 24
  • Most consecutive wins at the same Grand Prix: 5
  • Most pole positions at the same Grand Prix: 8
  • Most consecutive pole positions at the same Grand Prix: 7
  • Highest percentage of front row starts in a season: 100%
